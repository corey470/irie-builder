# DESIGN_DIRECTIONS.md — Irie Builder Design Toolkit

This file describes the style-reference layer inside Irie Builder.

The goal is not to clone reference brands. The goal is to translate their visual language into original websites that still fit the user's product.

## Core References

### Nike
- Massive type
- Full-bleed imagery
- Physical energy
- Dark contrast
- Motion that creates momentum
- Best for: streetwear, culture brands, athletic products, bold launches

### Apple
- Premium restraint
- Cinematic product framing
- Clean whitespace
- Precise polish
- Best for: luxury products, premium launches, quiet confidence

### Vercel
- Black-and-white precision
- Technical confidence
- Tight hierarchy
- Product clarity
- Best for: developer tools, SaaS, platforms, infrastructure

### Stripe
- Elegant conversion flow
- Product storytelling
- Soft depth
- Premium SaaS polish
- Best for: fintech, product marketing, polished service brands

### Framer
- Motion-first modernity
- Bold landing-page rhythm
- Design-forward composition
- Best for: creative brands, portfolios, launch pages, visually ambitious startups

### Notion
- Warm clarity
- Reading-first structure
- Soft minimalism
- Helpful, calm pacing
- Best for: editorial products, wellness, thoughtful brands, service businesses

### Spotify
- Dark immersion
- Youth-forward energy
- Culture-heavy identity
- Best for: music, events, creator brands, street culture, nightlife

## How Irie Builder Should Use Them

1. Use design direction as input, not as a template.
2. Let the direction affect:
- typography
- contrast
- grid rhythm
- image treatment
- motion energy
- section order
- CTA style
3. Blend directions when helpful.
4. Always keep the output original and aligned with the user's brand.

## Example Blends

- Nike + Apple = powerful but premium
- Vercel + Notion = technical but warm
- Framer + Nike = motion-heavy, culture-forward
- Stripe + Apple = polished, expensive-feeling product marketing
- Spotify + Nike = youth, energy, immersion
